Data layer

Every controller speaks a different language. Kelvex reads all of them, and the data is yours.

Underneath the dashboard is a normalized stream of your entire refrigeration estate. You can live in Kelvex, or treat it as the clean source of truth that feeds everything else you run.

One normalized stream

Racks, zones, temps, pressures, alarms, refrigerant.

Kelvex translates BACnet, Modbus, and the major OEM protocols into one consistent shape, so a suction pressure means the same thing whether it came from a Danfoss supervisor or an old standalone controller. Where your controllers are reachable, Kelvex connects to them directly. Where they are not, it still brings the data in reliably, with no gaps in the record when a connection drops. Everything stays read-only by default.

How it stays safe

Read-only by default, and never a standing door into your controls.

Read-only by default

Kelvex observes and records. It does not write to your setpoints, so connecting it can never change how a system runs.

No open path to your controls

When you do need to change something, you reach your controller's own screen from inside Kelvex in a session that opens, is logged, and closes. Nothing stays open between visits.

Auditable access

Every export, API call, and controller session is recorded against a user, so you always know who saw what and when.
